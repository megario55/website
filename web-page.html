e-tron GT

Speciality of audi e-tron GT:

The Audi e-tron GT is a battery electric executive car produced by Audi since late 2020 as part of the e-tron battery electric sub-brand, and the third fully electric car model, after e-tron and e-tron Sportback SUVs. Based on the J1 platform shared with the Porsche Taycan, the car went on sale in March 2021.

What is the range of a Audi e-tron GT?

In the case of the Audi e-tron GT (Porsche Taycan 4S cousin), it's 238 miles (383 kilometers) of EPA Combined range, while the Audi RS e-tron GT (Porsche Taycan Turbo cousin) is rated at 232 miles (373 km).

Q-series

Power of world

The 2.0-liter, two-turbocharged four-cylinder engine in the 40 TFSI generates 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, and the 45 TFSI engine produces 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines use Audi's Quattro all-wheel-drive system and an eight-speed automatic transmission.

A-series

A series are there in Audi:

Audi India's current portfolio comprises the likes of A4, A6 and A8 sedans, Q2, Q5 and Q8 SUVs, S5 Sportback, RS5, RS7 and RS Q8 performance oriented cars as well as e-tron, e-tron GT and RS e-tron GT electric cars. Audi follows a simple naming format, for the most part, employing letters and numbers. Base models have an “A” designation. Sportier luxury models get an “S.” The sportiest “RennSport” models get an “RS.” Audi uses “Q” for its SUV line.

RSQ3 series

It is the first production turbo model in a Q-series vehicle; based on the Audi RS Q3 concept. The car accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h (62 mph) in 5.5 seconds and onto a limited top speed of 250 km/h (155.34 mph).
